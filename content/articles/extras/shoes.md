# Shoes

_(This section is optional. You do not need a dedicated set of shoes for fencing as long as you have dedicated indoor shoes. A dedicated set of shoes for fencing can be beneficial though if you're all about optimization.)_

Most fencing gear will acquire will _not_ make you a better fencer. Sure, things that fit better or get in your way less will help, and having a sword that works with your style makes a big difference. Those caveats aside, you can't get a jacket that will score you more points, or a glove that will parry faster.

Shoes are an exception.

The good news is that you don't need fancy high-end shoes. What you're looking for are _court shoes._

## Running Shoes

Running shoes seem like a good default option for sports because they're athletic, and we're swinging swords and lunging at each other, which also sounds pretty athletic. Running shoes are meant to carry your momentum forward though. The cushion can shimmy a little bit so that when you land a running step, it's already propelling you forward. This may be the opposite of what you want when lunging in fencing.

## Court Shoes

Court shoes on the other hand are "sticky." They help your foot stay where you put it down. This can keep you more stable when making a lunge and give you better traction when slipping a leg or doing some bananas traverse. Court shoes can only do so much in the face of a slippery or dusty floor, but they'll still be an improvement.

If you're not sure which one to get, the current NHF favourite is the ASICS Gel Rocket, but realistically, any pair of court shoes will feel like an upgrade over running shoes. You can find these at sporting good stores like Sportchek, or the the ASICS store in the outlet mall.
