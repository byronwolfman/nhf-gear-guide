# Upper Arms and Shoulders

Jackets tend to become thinner at the forearms out of the expectation that protectors will be worn, whereas the upper arms and shoulders on non-"light" jackets tend to be thicker. You might be able to get away without additional upper arm and shoulder protection if all the following are true:

* You are not wearing a "light" variant of a jacket
* Your jacket's upper arms are sufficiently thick
* You are primarily fencing with single-handed weapons

Even if you don't _need_ additional upper arm protection, a big hit to the shoulder can still be uncomfortable, so these are still worth consideration.

_(See also [forearms and elbows](/articles/protection/forearms-and-elbows/).)_

## Arming Straps

Some jackets have what are called "arming straps" along their upper arm and shoulder areas. These are a series of small fabric loops that let you tie protectors in place. Some upper arm protectors require arming straps, some do not (if the protector's product photos don't show a strap encircling the whole arm, then arming straps are probably required).

Protectors that tie onto arming straps come with small lengths of elastic paracord that will eventually break or become lost. I'd recommend a journey to Fabricland to pick up a few feat of spare paracord and also packing a small set of scissors in your gear bag for makeshift repairs.

## Recommendations

### SPES

They don't look like much, but the SPES [PRO Set](https://histfenc.eu/en/men/97-pro-set-shoulders-and-upper-arms-5909378287547.html) upper arm/shoulder guards are surprisingly effective. these protect your outside upper arm where you are most likely to get hit, plus your shoulders. I would strongly encourage these at minimum for anyone wearing a "light" variant jacket, even for single-handed weapons, and especially for two-handed weapons.

These require arming straps.

### Superior Fencing

The [Heavy Arm Protector](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5297.html) looks interesting for longsword, but I've never seen this kit in the wild. It appears to be based on a design from a different (defunct) OEM. These do not require arming straps.

### MAC

MAC sells a fairly premium set of [shoulder protectors](https://www.mac-armour.cz/p/shoulder-protector). I've only encountered one of these in the wild. They seem to move well, and look great.

### New(er) Stuff

I haven't encountered anyone with these yet, but they're carried by reputable sellers.

North Armour has a new [whitelabel shoulder guard](https://northarmoury.ca/na-hema-shoulder-guard/) that looks interesting, and offers substantially more coverage than the more common SPES PRO Set. [HCA also carries them](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/shoulder-protectors). I'm unsure if these require arming straps.

There's also the Maestro Upper Arms/Shoulder set, [carried by North Armoury](https://northarmoury.ca/maestro-upper-arm-shoulder/) and also [carried by HCA](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/maestro-shoulder-and-upper-arm-protectors). I'm unsure if these require arming straps.
