# Knees and Shins

Legs are difficult to defend, and there's almost always at least one out in front of the body. Fortunately we have lots of options here, as knee and shin protection is not unique to HEMA, and the swords are no sharper than a hockey stick.

Some of these protectors come as combination shin + knees, and others as separate components that can be combined across brands.

## Recommendations

### Used Hockey Shins

The fastest, cheapest way to get shin protection is to see who in your neighbourhood is selling old hockey gear. The hardest part about buying used hockey shins is that the seller may try to sell you the rest of their kit too.

If you go this route, make sure that the protectors cover all the way down to your ankles (or at the very least don't leave a large gap between the protector and your foot). Hockey protectors may also expose the very top of your knee during a committed lunge. Mine have this problem actually, but it can be mostly mitigated if the protectors offer a way to tie them to an arming strap on your pants (a battle kilt or long skirt can also solve this problem).

Used baseball catcher shins is another quick route to leg protection.

If giant protectors aren't your thing, some fencers wear rugby shin guards under their socks, and only wear knee guards on the outside.

Because there aren't a lot of swords in hockey or baseball, these type of protectors do not tend to guard the back of the calf. Such strikes are uncommon, but sometimes happen during a horizontal traverse. Nevertheless, hockey leg and knee protectors are a very common sight at clubs and tournaments.

### Superior Fencing

The second-most-common knee and shin guards you'll see at clubs and tournaments are Supfen protectors. You can get their basic protectors from [Swordshop here](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/Basic-Shin-Guard/44) or directly from [Supfen here](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5292.html) if you're making a Supfen order at the same time.

Superior fencing has other models that seem more substantial, but I don't know them well enough to say how they perform in terms of protection and comfort. Their full line of protectors is [here](https://www.supfen.com/products/protectors.html) and you can sometimes find the non-basic shin guards at North Armoury and Swordshop.

### North Armoury and HCA

There's an interesting whitelabel shin and knee protector available from North Armoury [here](https://northarmoury.ca/na-hema-shin-and-knee-guard/) and HCA [here](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/knees-and-shins). One of our club members has these and they seem fairly good, although these did require some minor repair and modifications from what I understand. I would recommend getting a few feet of spare elastic paracord from Fabricland and a pair of scissors for your gear bag in case emergency repairs are needed.

### HF Armory

I've only seen these in the wild once on a particularly tall fencer. HF Armory makes a pair of [greaves](https://hf-armory.com/en/shop/protectors/chest-protection/greaves-protection-black-knight/) which can be paired with their [knee protection](https://hf-armory.com/en/shop/protectors/chest-protection/knee-protection-black-knight/).

### Plasticsmith Armoury

I haven't talked about this seller much because for the most part I don't know their work. Someone at my previous club had a pair of their [Deluxe Poleyn Graves](https://plasticsmitharmoury.com/products/deluxe-poleyn-greaves) and loved them.

Plasticsmith items are all made to order, so "out of stock" actually means the production queue is full at the time.

### KneePro

KneePro protectors protect only the knee, as the name implies. This protection extends both above and below the knee, and are hinged in such a way that there are no gaps even in a full lunge. You can get a lot of protection by combining these with shin guards.

Right now that only place I can find these are through Purpleheart Armoury [here](https://www.woodenswords.com/Ultra_Flex_III_Knee_Pads_p/or.kneepro.htm). I would strongly recommend only ordering from Purpleheart if you can get together a big group order, otherwise shipping and customs can really drive up the final cost.

### MAC

Caveat: I've not seen these in the wild yet, but I've heard good things about them, and the MAC stuff I have seen has been quite good.

MAC sells knee protectors in [regular](https://www.mac-armour.cz/p/knee-protectors) and [winged](https://www.mac-armour.cz/p/knee-protectors-winged) variants. These can be combined with their [shin protectors](https://www.mac-armour.cz/p/shin-protectors) or [greaves](https://www.mac-armour.cz/p/greaves), the latter of which are shin protectors with calf protection.
