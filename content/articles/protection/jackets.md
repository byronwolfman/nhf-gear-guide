# Jackets

If you haven't already, check out the page on [puncture resistance](/articles/overview/puncture-resistance/). If you want the short version though: 800N offers considerably more protection and is a good idea for longsword or rapier. 350N will not perform as well in the case of a broken blade, but can be sufficient for lighter single-handed weapons when combined with other protection.

## Tailoring

There's not much that can be done to tailor a jacket after-the-fact. These jackets are made from heavy materials that tend to break regular alteration machinery. Occasionally someone finds a a tailor willing and able to add zippers to the sleeve, but significant alterations tend not to be posisble. Many jackets have an adjustable strap on the back to tighten the waist, but that's about it.

That being the case, custom sizing might be important if

* You present as a woman
* You have somewhat unique proportions
* You've been betrayed at some point by a "unisex" t-shirt

Fencers who present as male or otherwise have very rectangular bodies can sometimes get away with off-the-shelf sizes like small/medium/large/xlarge/etc. If you're unsure about custom tailoring (or you're really, really sure you need it), consider opting for it at order time. It's beetter to pay a little more upfront to get something that fits, instead of waiting for several months for something that doesn't.

## Recommendations

We have a lot of options. The recommendations below are not exhaustive, and you can find jackets from many other [sellers](/articles/overview/where-to-buy/#list-of-sellers).

When possible, prefer jackets that offer "arming straps." Though arming straps are not necessary, if you are comparing two otherwise identical jackets and one has arming straps and the other does not, I recommend buying the jacket that has them. These will be described more in the [upper arms and shoulders](/articles/protection/upper-arms-and-shoulders/) section (but in short: they make it easier to attach upper overlays).

### Superior Fencing

[Superior Fencing Jackets](https://www.supfen.com/products/jackets.html) are inexpensive, decent quality, and come in a lot of different styles. Custom sizing is offered at no additional charge which you really should take advantage of. Err on the side of a relaxed fit when measuring; Supfen jackets are sometimes criticized as being a bit tight or less flexible than more "premium" brands. 

Supfen jackets are made to order and in my experience can take 3-6 months for production and shipping.

### SPES

[SPES jackets](https://histfenc.eu/en/) are some of the best jackets you can get _though you will pay for it_. They come in a number of styles, and can be heavily customized and tailored for an additional charge. Some jackets are also offered with a "light" variant made from thinner materials; speaking from experience, these are much more forgiving in summer months, but you will also bruise far more easily.

In my experience SPES jackets usually take 3-4 months for production and shipping. If you don't need custom sizing and want to roll the dice, you can also check out their [outlet](https://histfenc.eu/en/148-outlet) section.

### Swordshop

The whitelabelled [Ultralight Padded HEMA Jacket](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/Ultralight-Padded-HEMA-Jacket-350N/112) is an interesting jacket that lives up to its name. Our sabre instructor, Matt, has one, so you should talk to him if mobility is what's most important to you. As this is an off-the-shelf jacket, custom sizing isn't possible. I would probably not recommend this for longsword.

This jacket is not made on-demand, so if it's in stock, it will ship right away.

Swordshop stocks a [number of other jackets](https://www.swordshop.ca/shop/jackets/8), which appear to be Supfen models. These too will ship quickly when in stock.
