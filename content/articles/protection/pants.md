# Pants and Upper Legs

There are not as many options for pants as there are jackets, but maybe that's for the best, especially if you have been spending hours with the SPES jacket customizer. As with jackets
you'll want to be familiar with [puncture resistance](/articles/overview/puncture-resistance/) when choosing between 350N and 800N. The short version though is that 350N is better than nothing, and 800N offers substantially better protection.

## Tailoring

There's not much that can be done to tailor a pants after-the-fact. These pants are made from heavy materials that tend to break regular alteration machinery. Some pants have suspenders to keep them from falling down if the waist happens to be a bit loose, and skirts are often considered "universal fit" due to the adjustable waist. Otherwise, we're fairly limited in post-production modifications.

## Form Factors

One form factor for pants is, well, pants.

A lot of HEMA pants will go down to just above the knee. The reason they don't extend below the knee is because puncture resistant fabrics won't do anything to stop your opponent from breaking your shins. You are instead expected wear hard protection over your knees and shins, which will fit (and breath) more easily if you don't have heavy puncture-resistant fabric in the way.

Another common form factor is "puffy pants" or "pluderhose." These are pretty much exactly what they sound like, and they look fantastic, like a Meyer manual come to life. These also stop at the knee.

Last but not least are padded skirts and kilts. I've never tried these myself so I can't offer a substantive opinion. Some fencers prefer the mobility or aesthetic or both. There are long and short varieties, the latter of which may not necessarily be sufficient on its own.

## Recommendations

The recommendations below are not exhaustive, and you can find pants from many other [sellers](/articles/overview/where-to-buy/#list-of-sellers).

### Superior Fencing

The [Superior Fencing pants](https://www.supfen.com/products/pants.html) section offers pants, puffy pants, and skirts. There are a handful of really wild styles too. 

Supfen pants are made to order and in my experience can take 3-6 months for production and shipping.

### SPES

The [SPES legs](https://histfenc.eu/en/160-legs) section offers pants, puffy pants, and skirts. At this time of writing, most options are primarily functional. Customization and tailoring is available for an additional charge.

SPES pants are made to order and in my experience take 3-4 months for production and shipping.  If you don't need custom sizing and want to roll the dice, you can also check out their [outlet](https://histfenc.eu/en/148-outlet) section.

### Distributor Options

If you don't need custom sizing, you can sometimes find Supfen pants in stock in [North Armoury Leg Protection](https://northarmoury.ca/knee-leg-guards/) and [Swordshop Leg Protection](https://www.swordshop.ca/shop/leg-protection/10).
