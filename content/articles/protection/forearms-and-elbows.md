# Forearms and Elbows

A thick padded jacket isn't enough to protect your elbows from a direct strike (ask me how I know). Elbow protection is often combined with forearm protection, and should be worn on both left and right sides even if you're only using a one-handed weapon (your off hand usually isn't in danger, but that can change quickly if a blade grab goes wrong).

Forearm and elbow protectors are usually not needed for smallsword, but some tournament organizers may require one or both regardless.

_(See also [upper arms and shoulders](/articles/protection/upper-arms-and-shoulders/).)_

## Recommendations

### SPES

For single-handed weapons, the SPES [Geko](https://histfenc.eu/en/men/43-geko-forearm-and-elbow-overlays-5908433172460.html) and [Vectir](https://histfenc.eu/en/men/91-vectir-forearm-and-elbow-overlays-5900162169131.html) protectors are probably some of the best protectors you can get. They wrap around the whole arm, and the elbow protector attaches with velcro to provide overlapping coverage even with a bent elbow. I've done longsword sparring with them too, but since these protectors use several strips of plastic inserts instead of one or two big pieces, you'll still feel a hard hit from a longsword.

SPES also sell [PRO Set](https://histfenc.eu/en/men/98-pro-set-forearms-and-elbows-5900109353524.html) forearm guards which are designed as a single piece plastic shell for each elbow and forearm. On the one hand these definitely offer superior protection wherever the shell covers, but I don't like the gap they leave on the inside of the forearm. That's just a personal preference though -- there are definitely people using these.

### Superior Fencing

Supfen have a range of forearm guards. I've never encountered the styrogum protectors out in the wild so I haven't formed an opinion on whether or not they're good, so I might recommend sticking to the plastic guards for now.

[This protector](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5202.html) looks a lot like its SPES counterpart, but I'm not sure what the inserts are made of.

The [Heavy Forearm Elbow Protectors](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5296.html) will, as their namesake implies, take a heavy blow. I find these have less coverage than SPES Gekos and Vectirs, but crucially, bones are protected, so the reduced overall coverage may not be a problem.

The [Heavy Arm Protector](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5297.html) looks interesting for longsword, but I've never seen this kit in the wild. It appears to be based on a design from a different (defunct) OEM.

### Maestro

These are pretty new and I haven't seen them in person yet. At first glance, it looks like these provide coverage similar to SPES Geko/Vectir protectors, but are instead made from a few large pieces of plastic rather than several strips. It's hard to tell from the photos but the [North Armoury version](https://northarmoury.ca/maestro-forearm-elbow/) looks slightly different than the [HCA version](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/maestro-elbow-and-forearm-protectors).
