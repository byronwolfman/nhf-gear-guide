# Gloves

This is probably going to be one of the biggest pages so buckle up. The kind of gloves you wear is going to depend on the weapon you use, and outside of high-end and extremely specialized gloves, you will usually need a different pair for each weapon. Consequently, that's how I'm going to break down this page.

Note that it is impossible to detail every potentially good glove, so I'm not going to try! If the recommendations below aren't to your liking, check out the [list of sellers](/articles/overview/where-to-buy/) and if you see something interesting, look for a review by the [Academy of Historical Fencing](https://www.youtube.com/@AcademyofHistoricalFencing/videos). AHF is UK-based, meaning the meta leans towards slightly more heavy swords, so if they say a glove is good, it's probably good!

## Sabre (and sometimes Broadsword)

Sabre works best with a five-finger glove in order to switch between different grips and manage some of the more difficult cuts (looking at you, cut 3). It's also difficult to fit a mit into most sabre guard, and usually impossible to fit one into a broadsword's baskethilt.

Fingers are still exposed on either side of a sabre's guard (and especially if using a stirrup hilt) so a "medium" glove will be my minimum recommendation. Alternatively a broadsword encloses most of the hand inside a metal basket, so some additional light glove options open up. I'll do my best to point out where that's the case.

Here are some recommendations:

### Thokk Weaponmaster Gauntlets

These are high-quality "premium" gloves that offer good protection and dexterity and appear at a lot of tournaments. I haven't seen them carried by any Canadian distributors, so you will have to order them directly from Thokk. They come in [350N](https://www.thokk-research.com/product-page/weaponmaster-gauntlets-pro) and [800N "Blade Proof"](https://www.thokk-research.com/product-page/weaponmaster-gauntlets-bladeproof) variants.

These do not fit in a broadsword hilt.

### Superior Fencing Kevlar Gloves

I would classify these as somewhere in between light and medium gloves. This is one of the best glove choices for a baskethilted broadsword and if that's what you're using, you may as well go ahead and get these gloves. For sabre I'd personally opt for a different glove, but these can be enough if you're doing lighter intensity sparring.

You can get these from North Armoury [here](https://northarmoury.ca/sf-kevlar-gloves/), Swordshop [here](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/SF-HEMA-Kevlar-Gloves-with-Fingertip-Protectors/58), or directly from Supfen [here](https://www.supfen.com/product/gloves/glv1102.html).

Tournaments generally allow these gloves for use with broadswords. Some but not all tournaments allow these gloves for use with sabres.

### Superior Fencing Impact Resistant Gloves

Sometimes called "Thokk Offs," these gloves offer pretty good protection, and were my preferred glove for awhile. They do wear out faster than the real thing, and can be stiff to break in. I'm pretty sure these have saved me from at least a couple of broken finger incidents.

You can get the "v2" from [Swordshop](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/Supfen-Impact-Resistant-Gloves-2.0/104) or [Supfen directly](https://www.supfen.com/product/gloves/hfg0706.html).

You can get the "v3" from [North Armoury](https://northarmoury.ca/hema-impact-resistant-gloves-pro-dyneema/), [Swordshop](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/supfen-impact-resistant-gloves-3-0/126), or [Supfen directly](https://www.supfen.com/product/gloves/hfg0708.html).

These are generally allowed in tournaments for use with sabre. They do not fit in a broadsword baskethilt.

### CHFG Gabriel Gloves

Gabriels are a popular premium quality glove that are intended to be five finger _longsword_ gloves. That said, they'll work just fine with a sabre too. If you have the budget and are looking for one glove to rule them all, CHFG Gabriels can be a good choice. You can get them through [Swordshop](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/CHFG-Gabriel-Glove/82) and [HCA](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/chfg-gabriel-five-fingered-gloves).

These are generally allowed in tournaments for use with sabre. They do not fit in a broadsword baskethilt.

### HF Armory Black Prince

Black Princes are a premium quality glove with a few interesting quirks. They are smaller than many other hardshell five finger gloves, meaning they can fit inside smaller cups like the Sigi Sabre PRO. The gloves' resting position is a handshake grip, meaning you don't have to fight them to keep your grip closed when fencing. Be aware that these only come in a single size (M/L) which felt initially tight on my size large hands, but are now fine after the break-in period.

Black Prince gloves can be ordered [directly from HF Armory](https://hf-armory.com/en/shoppp/protectors/hand-protection/hema-gloves-black-prince/).

These are generally allowed in tournaments for use with sabre. Due to the plastics being thinner, they should never be used with longsword. These gloves did not fit in any broadsword hilt I've tried, though it was closer than I thought.

## Longsword

This really isn't my area of expertise so this section may be a bit bare until I have help filling it in.

Most mitten-style gloves will provide better protection than five finger gloves for longsword. Hand hits with longswords are common, and an armoured finger can still bend in a way it's not supposed to bend if hit hard enough. Longsword-specific five fingered gloves do exist which can offer better mobility with longsword techniques, and these gloves _can_ provide good protection, though for some additional risk.

If you're just starting out, I'd recommend starting with mitten style gloves. If you find a pair of five-fingered gloves you really like, you can always upgrade later.

Here are some recommendations:

### Superior Fencing Clamshell Gloves 2.0

I swear these are the workhorse of the longsword world. They are well priced (relatively speaking), offer great protection, and break in quickly. I have a pair of these for the odd occasion when I decide to pick up a longsword.

You can get these gloves from [North Armoury](https://northarmoury.ca/na-clamshell-gloves-2-0/), [Swordshop](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/Supfen-Clamshell-2.0/77), or directly from [Supfen](https://www.supfen.com/product/gloves/hfg0705.html).

These are generally allowed in tournaments.

### CHFG Gabriel Gloves

One of the most popular five-finger glove designed for longsword. As a bonus, they fit in most sabre hilts too. You can get them through [Swordshop](https://www.swordshop.ca/product/CHFG-Gabriel-Glove/82) and [HCA](https://www.historicalcombatarmory.com/product-page/chfg-gabriel-five-fingered-gloves).

These are generally allowed in tournaments.
