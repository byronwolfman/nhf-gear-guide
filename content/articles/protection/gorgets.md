# Gorgets

When fully kitted you should have at least three kinds of neck protection:

* Puncture resistance from your mask's bib
* A blade catcher in your jacket's collar
* Something stiff and rigid in front of your larynx

That last one is a gorget. At minimum they go around your neck, and a good ones can also provide some clavicle protection.

Gorgets can be a bit finnicky because they can interact with your jacket and mask in unpleasant ways. An ill-fitting gorget can make it difficult to turn your head up or down, or it can push your mask up, or otherwise just be uncomfortable. A well-fitting gorget doesn't even feel like it's there.

## Recommendations

### Superior Fencing

While most of these are recommendations for gear you _should_ consider, this one's an anti-recommendation. Supfen's [Articulated Heavy Gorget](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg2101.html) is indeed heavy, but its mobility is badly lacking. I do not recommend this gorget.

Supfen's more basic [HEMA Gorget](https://www.supfen.com/product/protectors/hfg5212.html) on the other hand has a good profile, offers some additional padded wings, and is easy to get on and off. Lots of us have this model. I'm not sure if these are stocked or made on-demand. North Armour sometimes stocks it as a whitelabel [North Armoury Gorget](https://northarmoury.ca/na-gorget/).

Some fencers prefer the minimalist [Roughneck Gorget](https://imexsport.ca/collections/hema/products/supfen-roughneck-gorget). Personally I prefer a bit more protection, but these absolutely do their job, and are accepted at tournaments. Consider a larger chest protector or shoulder protection if you opt for this one.

### Vytis

Though I'm not aware of any Canadian distributor carrying them (North Armoury did for awhile), Vytis Gorgets are considered high-quality. If you can find one, it's a good gorget.

### Winter Tree Crafts

Winter Tree Crafts makes a [Stainless Gorget](https://www.wintertreecrafts.com/items/gorget.html) which ought to hold its own against anything. I've only seen a couple in person, but the fencers that have them swear they're great. As WTC is a US-based OEM, exchange, shipping, and customs may significantly increase the final cost.
