# Longsword Recommendations

This will be sectioned out alphabetically by seller because there's a lot to write and I don't have a better idea. This isn't a list of every single longsword and seller, but rather a curated short list of longswords someone at my club has handled (and maybe even me). For a more exhaustive list, try opening every link in the big [seller's list](/articles/overview/where-to-buy/) and see what strikes your fancy.

## Aureus Swords | 🇵🇱

This doesn't really belong on a beginner's guide. The thing is, you're eventually going to notice John's longsword and wonder what it is, and it's an Aureus. These are premium swords with at least a 24 month wait according to their current production queue. In the meanwhile, order something else, and if you decide that longsword is your jam and you want the best one in the whole world, _then_ you can order an Aureus and start counting down the days.

Aureus swords are tournament legal.

## Esker Forge | 🇨🇦

The Esker Forge [Standard](https://eskerforge.com/product/the-standard/) is no longer available, though if you're lucky you might find someone willing to sell theirs. The Standard is a sword that cheats the laws of physics by using better physics. It is light and fast while offering the bind presence of a heavier sword. It is kinder both to you and your opponent. I know one fencer who, despite their criticisms of the Standard, still thought it was the best longsword they had ever handled.

The Standard is tournament legal, and considered lightweight according to the club's definitions.

## HF Armory | 🇺🇦

An HF Armory feder is a solid economy sword. Inexpensive without being cheap, and no-frills so you don't have to agonize over options. Sure, the Sigis of the world will catch eyes, but this sword can win tournaments too. You have your pick of [Classic](https://hf-armory.com/en/shop/weapon/long-swords/federschvert-classic/?alg_currency=EUR) or [Light](https://hf-armory.com/en/shop/weapon/long-swords/federschwert-slassic-light/?alg_currency=EUR). By our club's definition, the Classic is at the upper range of a medium-weight longsword, and the Light is at the upper range of a light-weight longsword.

Both are tournament legal.

## Sigi Forge | 🇸🇰

Just try to step away from Sigi's [Sword Builder](https://sigiforge.com/swordbuilder/). You can't! There are too many swords to detail here and the fun is exploring all the different ones available. In general though: Sigi feders tend to all hover around 1580g prior to customization (a heavy-weight longsword by our definition, but only just barely), and their light feders between 1250-1400g (a lightweight longsword by our definition).

Most (but not all) of their feders have schilts, so when presented with the choice, prefer one with a schilt.

Sigi swords tend to be tournament legal, but your customizations will ultimately determine that.
