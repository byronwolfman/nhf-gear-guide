# Sabre Recommendations

This will be sectioned out alphabetically by seller because there's a lot to write and I don't have a better idea. This isn't a list of every single sabre and seller, but rather a curated short list of sabres I've handled at some point. For a more exhaustive list, try opening every link in the big [seller's list](/articles/overview/where-to-buy/) and see what strikes your fancy.

## Arm Street | 🇺🇸

If you want to tinker with your sabre's performance, Arm Street has a couple of interesting sabres like the [Podium](https://armstreet.com/store/armoury/armstreet-podium-tournament-saber-hema-weapons) and [One Standard](https://armstreet.com/store/armoury/armstreet-one-standard-saber-hema-weapon). The idea is that you can swap out your sabre's guard or handle to change its weight or balance, all without having to go out and buy a whole new sabre. The blade itself is made by HF Armory.

These could be tournament legal or not depending on the modifications are in play. The blade would be considered a partial curve.

## Castille | 🇺🇸

If you're looking for a fun duelling sabre, there are a handful of Castille [Economy Dueling Sabre](https://castillearmory.com/product/complete-economy-dueling-sabre/) floating around the club. These are light, have a point-of-balance close to the hilt. They also used to be affordable, but the story's different now with the whole trade situation. One criticism I have for these swords is that the plastic grip doesn't have great traction.

If you're willing to order one regardless, I recommend the 20mm blade, as 16mm blades are more susceptible to break.

These are too light for some tournaments in Southern Ontario, but you might be able to sneak up on 700g minimums with a set of washers. If not attending a tournament, these are great for drilling and sparring non-stop.

The blade would be considered a partial curve.

## Krieger | 🇵🇱

One of our clubmates has a Krieger [S6](https://kriegerweapons.com/products/hema/s6-hema-sabre/). It's a robust, has good presence for its weight, and overall gets the job done. This sabre has medalled. The grip's texture can feel a bit strange on a bare hand, it this same texture also gives it excellent grip in a glove.

These should be legal in most if not all Ontario tournaments. This blade would be considered a full curve.

## Kvetun | ??

Kvetun is in a weird place right now and it isn't really possible to order swords from them at the moment. Some resellers may still have stock.

I have an Easton Mk3 sabre and it can be a lot of fun, but the models have become heavier over time. Mine weighs 855g at a time when the website still advertised it as an 800g sword. More significantly, the Easton blades have a large dumbbell profile which is seemingly indestructible, but can be unkind to the person on the receiving end. At least one Ontario tournament organizer banned Eastons last year, though the ban does not appear to have spread much at this time of writing.

Eastons were available in straight, partial curve, and full curve when orders were open. When not banned for being an Easton specifically, they are otherwise tournament legal.

## North Armoury | 🇨🇦

Someone at our club got their hands on a [WuWu Dueling Sabre](https://northarmoury.ca/dueling-sabre/) sold through North Armoury. It's a good economy sword that's fun to swing around. There seem to be a couple different versions of this sabre with different weights in the range of 710-730g, but regardless, those are tournament-legal weights.

The blade would be considered a partial curve.

## Regenyei Armory | 🇭🇺

It seems impossible to mention Regenyei without bringing up the fact that their _old_ sabres are considered crowbars, and that their current line is incredibly good. Sure enough, one of our clubmates has a Regenyei sabre, and it's pretty fun (and kind).

Cupped regenyei sabres come in three versions: the [Tournament](https://regenyei.com/product/torunament-saber/), the [Light](https://regenyei.com/product/light-saber/), and the [Duelist](https://regenyei.com/product/duelist-saber/). The Tournament and Light are tournament legal (though it's a close thing with the Light if its weight varies more than advertised) whereas the Duelist is not.

All blades would be considered partial curve.

## Sigi Forge | 🇸🇰

Unfortunately Sigi's Sabre PRO model isn't available at the moment, but more on that in a moment. The next best thing, the [Sabre Big](https://sigiforge.com/products/sigi-saber-big/) is a cuphilt sabre with enough room to accomodate truly enormous gloves. I wouldn't advise putting clamshells in there, but if you really want to, I bet you can.

The weight and handling of Sigi sabres depends a lot on what kind of blade you put on it. If you're looking for a specific target weight, you may want to email ahead and find out which combination will get you there, especially if you are looking for something tournament legal (weight aside, the rest of its specifications are tournament legal).

Sigi blades are sometimes criticized as being a bit wobbly. This makes them a bit kinder in a full-rainbow thrust, but you'll need to have good edge alignment for your binds and parries.

Somewhat confusingly, the "straight" blade option is a partial curve. The "curved" blade option is a full curve.

(The Sabre PRO is a currently unavailable model with a pecoraro hilt that typically only fits light gloves or HFA Black Prince gloves. It's a really fun sabre which will hopefully return to production one day.)

## Swordshop | 🇨🇦

Now this one's interesting. This is a swordshop design made in partnership with other sword makers, and not available anywhere else. I haven't had a chance to handle one, but it looks great on paper and has a [glowing review from HFA](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PWCOVWC_nro). There may be a club order for these in our future.

It's tournament legal and the blade would be considered a partial curve.
