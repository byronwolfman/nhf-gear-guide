# Where to Buy

Where you order from can mean the difference between shipping in days versus months, and predictable delivery rates versus surprise customs. Sometimes this is avoidable for common gear that shows up all over, and you can optimize ordering from a local supplier. Other times, there's only one distribution channel, and you either have to pay its freight (figuratively and literally) or pick something else.

## OEMs and Distributors

There are (broadly speaking) two major sources of HEMA gear:

1. Original equipment makers (OEMs)
1. Distributors

**OEMs** generally make equipment to order, can have multi-week or multi-month production queues, and sometimes can offer various customizations. Most OEMs have enough demand that they do not carry an inventory of ready-to-ship items, though there are exceptions. Most OEMs are outside of Canada, meaning that one must account for currency exchange, shipping, and the possibility of customs/import fees. Customs enforcement is wildly inconsistent, so hope for the best and plan for the worst.

**Distributors** on the other hand are more like storefronts that stock ready-to-ship gear. Some distributors stock the same equipment you would otherwise buy from OEMs, sometimes whitelabelled, and often at competitive pricing because they can bulk order. Stock tends to ebb and flow, and when something is in stock it is almost always the fastest way to get something. These tend _not_ to be drop-shippers, since they must bulk order their inventory to achieve margin and overcome their import costs.

There is some blurring of the lines where distributors have entered into partnership with swordmakers, but the distinction mostly holds.

Below is a non-exclusive list of OEMs and distributors that I would generally consider trustworthy. It is not an exhaustive list, and any omission does not mean a seller is untrustworthy. There is one exception:  I would strongly recommend avoiding Amazon or similar online megastores with marketplace sellers. This is one area where you don't want to roll the dice on counterfeits.
